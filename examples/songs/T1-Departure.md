## Complete Arrangement Design

### 1. Intro "Morning Room" Design (0:00-0:30)

- 0:15: Piano left hand octave bass enters, like footsteps slowly approaching
- 0:22: **Ambient sound slightly rises** — distant bird calls, hinting at the "departure" call
- Emotion: trapped → longing to depart

### 2. Verse1 "I Walked My Cage Into the Sky" Design (0:30-1:20)

**Vocal enters a cappella + piano bed**

- Piano: Minimal chord bed, only necessary support
- 0:50: Strings (violin) join very lightly, sustained notes

### 4. Hook1 "Walk Into the Sky" Design (1:40-2:15)

**Drum beat + electronic sounds + harmony explosion**

> **Hook variant × vocal timbre matrix (three-tier progression)**

| Time | Variant Lyrics | Vocal Processing | Timbre Goal | Arrangement |
|------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|-------------|
| 1:40 | wal**k into** sky | Bright primary, moderate chest resonance | Light, hopeful | Drums just entering, kick drum light |
| 1:50 | wal**k out of** sky | Mixed ratio increases, head resonance rises | Fuller, breakthrough feeling | Snare adds fills, strings gradually strengthen |
| 2:00 | st**ep into** sky | Breathy + low-frequency resonance (chest dive), slightly hoarse | Physical sensation, grounded feeling, struggle→flight texture | Full band, three-part harmony explodes |

**Key design points:**
- Progression from variant 1→2 achieved through **resonance transfer** (chest→head), no additional effects needed
- Progression from variant 2→3 is the **core highlight** of this Hook group: actively giving up some brightness for "stepping into" physical sensation
- Slight hoarseness in variant 3 is not a defect, but **poetic authenticity** — the sound of truly "stepping into the sky" naturally carries gasps

- 1:55: Three-part harmony explodes, full chord opens
- 2:08: **First emotional peak**, then slightly recedes to breathe for Verse2

### 6. Verse2 "Step By Step Mail My Body Back to the Scene" Design (2:35-3:30)

### 7. Hook2 "Departure Isn't Running Away, It's Admitting" Design (3:30-4:10)

**Full band + vocal silence (extended to 40 seconds)**

> **Hook2 silence × vocal vulnerability design**

> Core is not explosion, but "admitting" vulnerability. The vocal processing principle for this silence section: **as little processing as possible, expose as much truth as possible**.

**Technical specifications (3:42-3:50):**

| Parameter | Specification | Purpose |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Mic distance | Very close (<15cm), direct record | Capture breathy detail and sense of breath |
| Reverb | **Completely off** (dry vocal) | Only completely dry sound in the entire song, creating huge contrast with surroundings |
| Dynamics | No compression, no limiting | Allow "admitting" naturally dynamic fluctuation |
| EQ | Slight high-pass (<80Hz), no boosts | Remove extremely low frequency noise, preserve vocal character |
| Harmony | **Zero harmony** | Solo, sense of solitude |
| Piano coordination | Single note falls lightly, reverb ≥5s | Vocal dry/piano wet, spatial contrast |

**Special processing for "admitting" two characters:**
- Pronunciation weighted, sense of "swallowing" (not theatrical, truly speaking)
- "Is" slightly drags beat, like convincing oneself
- No breath between the two characters, one breath说完, sense of holding it in

- 3:30: Full track enters: drums + bass + electric guitar + strings + synthesizer
- 3:42: **Core emotional turning point — vocal silence (drums pause 2 beats)**
  - At "departure isn't running away is admitting": all instruments gradually weaken, only vocal solo remains
  - 1 beat after silence: piano single note falls lightly
- 3:50: **Gradual return**, "feet are wings" all parts gradually return
- 4:00: "I've been walking all along" reaches full song emotional peak

> **Outro tail dissipation × precise processing (4:03-4:10)**

| Parameter | Original | Round8 Optimization | Reason |
|-----------|----------|---------------------|---------|
| Reverb | None (dry) | **Long reverb (6-8s), but reverb itself doesn't fade** | "Dissipation" isn't abrupt cut-off, but sound "dissolving into air"; dry too short, no "sky" extension |
| Tail envelope | Natural decay | Slight artificial slow release (last 0.5s -3dB soft拐点) | Prevent digital audio noise amplification at ultra-low levels |
| Fade | None | **Pure reverb tail (Fader stays put, only reverb naturally disappears)** | Key: Fader doesn't move, let reverb naturally "leak" away — this is professional mixing's "controlled lack of control" |
| Piano tail | Single note falls lightly | Piano enters 0.5s before vocal, deep reverb (8s+) | Piano "sees vocal off," after vocal disappears piano reverb alone floats away, deeper lingering |

**Specific timing (4:03-4:10):**
- 4:03: Vocal "ground" exit
- 4:03.2: Piano single note enters (0.2s after vocal)
- 4:03.5: Vocal reverb begins (6-8s decay)
- 4:04.5: Vocal almost gone, piano reverb continues alone
- 4:10: Piano reverb naturally fades out (Fader never moves throughout)

### Rhyme Distribution

| Rhyme | Sound | Position | Emotional Texture |

| **closed rhyme** | chàng / shāng | Hook2 ending | Release after suppression |

### Hook Rhyme Design Logic

**Core strategy: Strong bind Hook, unified rhyme; Verse scattered, Arrangement uses rhythm patterns to differentiate emotion.**

1. **Hook1 rhyme (walk into sky):**
   - All three appearances rhyme with **-kong** (ong), with three different verbs (into/out/step), maintaining rhythmic unity while adding physical progression:
     - "walk **into** sky" → from nothing to something, starting point feeling
     - "walk **out of** sky" → breaking through boundary, sense of action
     - "st**ep into** sky" → physical grounding feeling, final connection to earth
   - Arrangement coordination: Three variants correspond to drum tension progression (1:40 enter → 1:50 snare fills → 2:00 full), vocal harmonies gradually layer

2. **Hook2 rhyme (departure isn't running away, is admitting):**
   - Rhyme loose, primarily colloquial rhythm, but **-ang** rhyme from "wings/wounds/windows" connects poetry, forming emotional contrast with Hook1's **-kong**:
     - Hook1 = sense of direction (walk→sky)
     - Hook2 = sense of admitting (struggle→reconciliation)
   - Arrangement coordination: 3:42 vocal silence, only piano single note, rhyme "admitting" with dragged note + light reverb, most exposed moment of entire song

3. **Verse rhyme (no rhyme/scattered):**
   - Verse1/2 don't rhyme at end, use **internal rhyme** and **imagery rhyme** instead (starlight/runway/sole/direction), piano/guitar rhythm patterns compensate for lack of rhyme
   - Highly matched with Verse a cappella + minimal arrangement design: "incomplete" in text, piano left-hand octave bass "completes" musically

### come/go Contrast Design (Dialectics in Rhyme)

> "Each step confirms the direction I **came** from"
> "Take myself apart, then put it **back**"
> "Every departure is a **return**"

The opposition and unity of 来 (lái) and 去 (qù) forms the **departure = return** philosophical proposition at the rhyme level. The 1:35 **1-beat very short silence** between Verse1→Hook1, exactly after the "去" character, is the auditory visualization of this rhyme tension.

### Hook1 and Verse2 Rhyme Landing Conflict (Ingenious Design)

| Section | Rhyme landing | Chord root (speculated) | Perception |
|---------|---------------|------------------------|------------|
| Verse1 end | "direc**tion**" (xiàng) | V or iv | Suspended, awaiting resolution |
| Hook1 enters | "walk into **sky**" (kōng) | I (B) | Returns to tonic, sense of release |
| Verse2 end | "**map**" (tú) | V | Suspended again |
| Hook2 enters | "**wings**" (bǎng) | I | Returns again, closed loop |

This **suspend→resolve→suspend→resolve** rhyme rhythm completely synchronizes with emotional arc "trapped→walk→fly→admit". Every Hook entry is "landing" for both rhyme and emotion, while Verse is the runway before flight.
